Switching to Middleman

28 Nov 2013

After a few days of exploratory surgery, I've decided to switch to Middleman. Liquid templates just don't give me the freedom and flexibility I want. I'm not sure why you'd want to use Liquid templates to make a website- surely Liquid templates are better suited for user-generated dynamic pages? Or perhaps the creators just wanted the simplicity.

It came down between nanoc and middleman. Middleman is much more active as a project, so that's the one I'm taking for now. I'm sure I'll change my mind sooner or later. But in truth, I'm really looking for a solution that's a little more generic than blogging, or even static website generating. No, actually I'm looking for some sort of file cruncher which consumes templates and outputs formatted data that would otherwise be tedious to maintain by hand. Jekyll is made for blogging and that's about it. Nanoc and Middleman are a bit more broad and flexible but I have yet to find the application I'm truly looking for.
